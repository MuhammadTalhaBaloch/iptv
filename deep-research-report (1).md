# Legal IPTV Playlist and EPG Sources for Jellyfin

## Executive summary

This inventory compiles a high-confidence, English-language CSV of **publicly available legal/community IPTV playlist, EPG, and metadata sources** that are relevant to Jellyfin and similar self-hosted clients. The strongest current sources for broad playlist coverage are **IPTV-org**, **Free-TV**, **freecasthub/public-iptv**, and **IPTV-FreeLegalCountries**; the strongest current guide-data sources are **EPGShare01**, **globetvapp/epg**, and the still-active **i.mjh.nz XML/JSON mirror files**. For FAST-platform-specific playlists, the clearest currently maintained replacement for older mjh.nz direct playlist links is **BuddyChewChew/app-m3u-generator**, while **Matt Huisman’s newer Samsung TV Plus tooling** and **Apsattv** provide additional active alternatives. Historical mjh.nz playlist URLs for Pluto TV, Samsung TV Plus, Plex, PBS, Roku, and Stirr are included but marked **archived** because Matt Huisman’s pinned August 2024 DMCA notice states those playlists were removed, even though related EPG/XML files and newer generators remain available. citeturn15search0turn22view3turn16view1turn16view2turn24search0turn24search1turn25search1turn25search4turn26search0turn39view1turn39view2turn39view0

This report **intentionally omits** private/paid IPTV subscriptions, Xtream/Stalker portals, pirate restream dumps, and commercial IPTV resale communities. In particular, results that pointed to **Rapid IPTV / iptv-community** were excluded because they are commercial/private and therefore outside the user’s requested “publicly available, legal” scope. citeturn18search1turn18search7

## Scope and methodology

I prioritized **primary project pages, official repository READMEs, indexed raw-file patterns, and official broadcaster/public-TV pages**. Where a source was clearly a player, proxy, extractor, or self-hosted generator rather than a playlist provider, I still included it as **`source_type=metadata`** because the user explicitly asked to carry forward entries such as **Awesome-iptv, Streamlink, IPTVnator, ErsatzTV, and Threadfin**. citeturn19view0turn21view0turn22view3turn16view1turn16view0turn11view3turn12view0turn13search10turn43view0

The fields **`license_or_notes`** and **`last_known_status`** are best-effort normalizations from source pages, not a legal opinion about every downstream channel URL or geo-rights situation. Community projects such as Free-TV, freecasthub, EPGShare01, Apsattv, and app-m3u-generator all make explicit claims about limiting themselves to free/legal/public material or public upstream sources, but those claims still depend on upstream services and can change over time. citeturn22view3turn16view1turn24search0turn39view0turn41view0

## Coverage highlights

The dataset below separates the ecosystem into four practical clusters. The “broad playlist” group is best when you want a single import URL or country/category/language splits. The “EPG” group is best when you already have playlists but need XMLTV coverage. The “FAST-specific/community alternatives” group matters most if you previously depended on mjh.nz playlist URLs for Pluto, Plex, Samsung TV Plus, Roku, PBS, or Stirr. The “tooling” group is there because those projects do not provide channels by themselves, but they are often part of a Jellyfin-ready workflow. citeturn15search0turn22view0turn16view1turn16view2turn23view0turn16view0turn25search1turn26search0turn41view0turn11view3turn12view3turn13search15turn43view0

## Open questions and limitations

A few rows are intentionally marked **`archived`** or **`unknown`** because the public evidence is mixed. The clearest example is the old **mjh.nz direct playlist** family: those URLs were widely used in older setup guides and community posts, but Matt Huisman’s pinned August 2024 issue says Pluto, Samsung, Stirr, Plex, PBS, and Roku playlists were removed after a DMCA complaint, so I treated them as historical rather than current despite still-active mirror activity on related XML/JSON files. citeturn26search0turn36search1turn36search2turn25search4turn35search4turn35search5

The **official single-feed examples** are included conservatively. TRT World, NHK World, and Al Jazeera public HLS URLs were surfaced by community issue/file references, while official site pages confirm those broadcasters maintain live public viewing pages; however, direct MPEG/HLS manifests can change without notice, so these are best treated as convenient examples rather than permanent stable contracts. NASA and LG were therefore recorded as official **metadata/portal** entries instead of forcing a speculative direct playlist URL. citeturn29search0turn29search2turn29search1turn14search2turn14search3turn14search0turn14search5turn38search7

Finally, some tools the user explicitly asked to include — **IPTVNator, Threadfin, ErsatzTV, Streamlink** — are not content sources. They are still useful in a Jellyfin workflow, but they belong in this CSV as **metadata/tooling rows**, not as direct playlist providers. citeturn11view3turn12view3turn13search10turn13search15turn43view0
